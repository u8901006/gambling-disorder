# Pathological Gambling / Gambling Disorder Research Toolkit

**Version:** 2026-05-08  
**Scope:** Medical, neuroscience, psychology, sociology, nutrition, and sports/exercise medicine literature related to pathological gambling, gambling disorder, problem gambling, gambling-related harms, online gambling, sports betting, and behavioral addictions.  
**Primary use:** Copy-ready search strings for PubMed and other scholarly search engines.

---

## 1. Terminology Map

### 1.1 Core diagnostic terminology

| Term | How to use it in searches | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Gambling disorder** | Primary modern clinical term | DSM-5/DSM-5-TR and ICD-11 terminology. Use for recent psychiatric, addiction, and clinical studies. |
| **Pathological gambling** | Legacy term; essential for older literature | DSM-IV term and still common in older PubMed records, MeSH entry terms, and older psychology literature. |
| **Problem gambling** | Broader public health and subclinical term | Often used in epidemiology, prevention, social science, screening, and policy literature. |
| **Disordered gambling** | Broad research term | Often captures clinical and subthreshold gambling problems. |
| **Compulsive gambling** | Lay and older clinical term | Useful for grey literature, older books, patient-facing materials, and some treatment studies. |
| **Gambling addiction** | Popular and addiction-framework term | Useful in addiction and behavioral addiction literature, but may retrieve non-clinical material. |
| **Gambling-related harm / gambling harms** | Public health and policy term | Important for sociology, public health, health economics, family impact, debt, crime, suicide, and regulatory research. |
| **Behavioral addiction / behavioural addiction** | Umbrella construct | Useful for journals and studies comparing gambling disorder with gaming disorder, internet addiction, compulsive buying, or substance use disorders. |
| **Sports betting / sports wagering** | Sports medicine, public health, and policy term | Essential for sports-related gambling research, especially athletes, adolescents, advertising, and online betting. |
| **Online gambling / internet gambling / mobile gambling** | Digital gambling term | Use for platform-based gambling, mobile betting, gambling apps, esports betting, and digital marketing. |

## 23. Search Quality Checklist

Before concluding that no evidence exists, check:

- Did you search both **gambling disorder** and **pathological gambling**?
- Did you include **problem gambling** for subclinical/public health studies?
- Did you include British spelling: **behavioral / behavioural**?
- Did you include both **sports betting** and **sports wagering**?
- Did you include **online gambling**, **internet gambling**, and **mobile betting**?
- Did you search both PubMed and psychology/social-science databases?
- Did you check review papers first?
- Did you run citation-chaining in Semantic Scholar or Google Scholar?
- Did you verify DOI metadata in Crossref?
- Did you search grey literature for policy and public-health reports?
- Did you use older terms for older literature and newer DSM/ICD terms for recent literature?
